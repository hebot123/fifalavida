USA’s Opening Match in Los Angeles:
A Smart Traveler’s Guide to FIFA World Cup 26™

By FIFA La Vida Travel Advisor

On June 12, 2026, the U.S. Men’s National Team will kick off its World Cup campaign at SoFi Stadium in Inglewood, part of the greater Los Angeles area. LA will host the USA’s first and third group-stage matches and a quarterfinal later in the tournament – making it one of the key hubs of FIFA World Cup 26™.

Snapshot: USA’s LA Opener at a Glance

Date Friday, June 12, 2026
Match USA’s Opening Group-Stage Game (Match 4 – Group D, USA #1)
Stadium SoFi Stadium (Los Angeles Stadium), Inglewood, CA
City Los Angeles Metropolitan Area
Time Zone Pacific Time (UTC-7 in June)
Other LA Matches Multiple group-stage games (including USA’s third match), Round of 32, and a Quarterfinal

Think of Los Angeles not just as a match venue, but as a month-long World Cup playground: fan festivals, watch parties, beach days, and late-night celebrations spread across one of the world’s most iconic metropolitan areas.

Why Traveling for This Match Is Different From a Regular Trip to LA

Most LA guides assume you’re here for Hollywood and the beaches. World Cup visitors have a very different reality: tight match-day schedules, huge crowds converging on a single venue, and a massive global audience focused on one game.

Key differences World Cup travelers need to plan around:

  • Event-level security: Entry procedures will be closer to a major international final than a typical club match. Expect multiple checkpoints and longer walking routes.
  • Stadium campus, not just a building: SoFi is part of a larger entertainment district (Hollywood Park, Kia Forum, Intuit Dome). That means controlled traffic flows, designated fan zones, and restricted parking on match days.
  • LA’s geography: The city is spread out. A hotel “in Los Angeles” can still be 60–90 minutes from the stadium at rush hour.
  • Microclimates: June can bring “June Gloom” along the coast (cool, cloudy mornings) while inland feels dry and warm. Pack layers, not just jerseys.

Booking Smart: Flights, Visas, and Timing for the USA Opener

1. When to arrive (and when not to)

  • Arrive 2–4 days before June 12 if you’re crossing more than 3–4 time zones. Jet lag plus LA traffic plus match-day stress is not a fun combination.
  • Avoid landing the morning of the match even if the schedule “technically works.” Immigration queues, baggage delays, and freeway traffic make this a high-risk move.
  • Consider staying through USA’s third group match (also in LA). You can base yourself in one hotel and avoid the extra moving around.

2. Entry & visa tips international fans often overlook

The U.S. is introducing a World Cup–priority visa and interview system, but a ticket will not guarantee entry. Treat visas like a separate project that starts early.

  • If you’re from a Visa Waiver Program country, confirm your ESTA status and passport validity (many airlines require 6 months validity beyond your stay).
  • Non–Visa Waiver travelers: schedule your visa appointment well in advance. Do not assume that World Cup–related fast-track systems will be available in your city or at your preferred time.
  • Carry proof of match tickets, accommodation, and return flights. Border officials may ask for evidence of your plans and ability to support yourself.

3. Flight strategy: think airport + neighborhood, not just “LAX”

  • Primary airport: Los Angeles International (LAX) is the closest major hub to SoFi (about 10–20 minutes by car without heavy traffic).
  • Consider nearby airports (BUR (Burbank), SNA (Orange County)) if fares are lower – but factor in 60–90 minutes of extra transfer time.
  • Multi-city tickets (e.g., arriving in LA and departing from San Francisco, Seattle, or New York after other matches) can sometimes cost only a bit more than a simple round-trip.

Where to Stay: LA Neighborhoods & Hotels That Actually Work for the Match

Don’t just search “Los Angeles hotel” and sort by price. For the USA opener, you want a base that balances: stadium access, overall safety and comfort, and things to do on non-match days.

Option A – Stadium District & Inglewood: Walk or Short Ride to SoFi

If your number one priority is minimizing match-day logistics, stay near the stadium. Inglewood is not LA’s most picturesque neighborhood, but it’s improving and extremely convenient for games and concerts.

  • The Lüm Hotel Los Angeles Stadium District – Modern, SoFi-adjacent hotel with rooftop bar and pool. Roughly a 15–20 minute walk or short rideshare to SoFi, Intuit Dome, and Kia Forum.
  • Elev8 Hotel Los Angeles LAX – Value-friendly option within walking distance of SoFi (about 20 minutes) with straightforward, no-frills comfort.
  • Hampton Inn / Best Western / mid-range chains in the Inglewood/Hawthorne area – a good balance of price and practicality if you just need a clean, reliable base near the stadium.

Pro tip: book flexible rates. Stadium-area hotels will adjust prices as the tournament approaches and may fill with official delegations or sponsor groups.

Option B – LAX / Century Boulevard: Airport Convenience + Quick Stadium Access

The hotel strip along Century Boulevard near LAX is a surprisingly strong choice for World Cup fans: constant shuttles, plenty of food options, and a short drive to SoFi.

  • Hyatt House LAX / Century Blvd – Apartment-style suites, rooftop spaces, free breakfast, and quick access to SoFi and Manhattan Beach.
  • Hyatt Place LAX / Century Blvd, Los Angeles Airport Marriott, Sheraton Gateway, Embassy Suites – full-service and select-service airport hotels with frequent shuttles, gyms, and on-site dining.

Insider angle: staying at LAX can shorten both your arrival and departure days and still keep you within 15–25 minutes of the stadium in normal traffic.

Option C – Beach Cities (Manhattan, Hermosa, El Segundo): World Cup by the Ocean

If you want to mix World Cup energy with classic California beach life, base yourself in the South Bay: Manhattan Beach, Hermosa Beach, or El Segundo.

  • Expect about 25–40 minutes by car to SoFi outside of peak traffic – more if traveling during Friday rush hour.
  • Look for mid- to upper-mid-range hotels and apartments within walking distance of the beach and the restaurants clustered along the main streets.
  • Westdrift Manhattan Beach (Marriott Autograph), boutique beach hotels, and well-reviewed chain properties in El Segundo are strong options if budget allows.

Pro tip: on match day, go to SoFi early, then return to the beach for late-night celebrations.

Option D – Urban Hubs (Culver City, West Hollywood, Santa Monica): Food, Nightlife, and Culture

If you see the match as one highlight in a broader LA vacation, consider staying in a more central or nightlife-oriented neighborhood.

  • Culver City – Great restaurants, bars, and easy access both to the Westside and to Inglewood. A smart “all-rounder” base.
  • Santa Monica – Classic pier, beach, shopping, and coastal vibe. Plan at least 35–50 minutes to SoFi on match days.
  • West Hollywood / Hollywood – Nightlife, rooftop pools, and easy access to Hollywood attractions; match-day travel could be 40–60 minutes each way.

Reality check: you’re trading convenience for lifestyle here. Great if you’re in town for 5–7 days; less ideal if you’re on a very tight schedule.

Getting to SoFi Stadium: Transport, Timing, and Exit Strategies

1. Rideshare done right

  • Use Uber/Lyft or other app-based services and plan to be dropped at designated rideshare zones – these may be a 10–20 minute walk from your gate.
  • For the opener, build in a generous buffer: arrive at the stadium area 2–3 hours before kick-off to allow for security and to enjoy the atmosphere.
  • After the match, walk 10–20 minutes away from the stadium before requesting a ride to avoid the worst traffic and surge pricing.

2. Parking & driving tips many visitors miss

  • Pre-book parking if you intend to drive; SoFi often uses a fully reserved parking system for major events.
  • Check official guidance on tailgating, bag rules, and arrival windows. Policies can differ between NFL games and FIFA matches.
  • Consider parking further away (at a mall or transit hub where it’s allowed) and taking a short rideshare to the stadium to avoid gridlock right at kickoff/exit time.

3. Public transit & shuttles

LA is expanding transit options around SoFi, including light-rail lines and event shuttles. Details will be updated as the tournament approaches.

  • Monitor official SoFi Stadium and LA World Cup websites for dedicated World Cup shuttle routes and special Metro schedules.
  • If you’re comfortable with transit, combining Metro rail + shuttle + short walk can be more predictable than driving in heavy traffic.

Inside SoFi Stadium: Small Details That Make a Big Difference

  • Roofed, but not fully enclosed: SoFi has a massive translucent roof and open sides. You’re protected from direct sun or drizzle, but temperatures and breezes still feel like outdoors. Dress in layers.
  • Food & drink strategy: Concessions are high quality but busy. Eat a light meal before entering, then use quieter early windows to grab stadium snacks instead of going right at halftime.
  • Walk the concourse once: SoFi’s design is part of the experience – take one loop early to find restrooms with shorter lines, quieter corners, and your favorite food stall.
  • Noise & energy: A USA home World Cup match will be loud. Earplugs are a smart idea for kids or anyone sensitive to sound.
  • Leave-time decision: Either commit to leaving a bit early (if the result is decided) or plan to stay and linger in the stadium area afterward. The worst experience is rushing out simultaneously with 70,000 people.

What to Do Around LA: Build a 3–5 Day World Cup City Break

Day 1 – Land, Reset, Explore a Neighborhood

  • Check into your hotel, shower, and walk the neighborhood – don’t jump straight onto the freeway.
  • If you’re near the beach, stroll the Manhattan Beach Pier or Hermosa Beach Strand.
  • In Culver City or West Hollywood, keep it simple: a casual dinner, early night, and lots of water.

Day 2 – Classic LA Sights (Without Overdoing It)

  • Morning at Santa Monica Pier & beach or Venice Boardwalk.
  • Afternoon visit to The Getty Center (art + views) or Griffith Observatory (city skyline, Hollywood sign).
  • Light evening: sunset drinks, then early bed before match day.

Day 3 – Match Day: USA’s Opening Game

  • Slow morning. Hydrate, eat a balanced breakfast, and keep alcohol to a minimum before the game.
  • Head toward SoFi early, planning to be through security at least 90–120 minutes before kickoff.
  • After the match, celebrate in Inglewood, Culver City, or wherever your base is – avoid deciding “where to go” only once you’re stuck in stadium traffic.

Day 4+ – Food, Neighborhoods, and Extra Matches

  • Sample LA’s food scene: Korean BBQ in Koreatown, tacos in East LA or the Valley, dim sum in the San Gabriel Valley.
  • Explore neighborhoods like Silver Lake, Echo Park, or Los Feliz for indie shops, coffee, and bars.
  • If you have tickets to other LA matches, treat each game as an anchor point and build half-day itineraries around them.

Practical Checklist: Things USA Opener Travelers Forget

Documents & Money

  • Check passport validity and visa/ESTA requirements months ahead.
  • Notify banks and enable international transactions on your cards.
  • Carry at least one backup card in a separate wallet or bag.
  • Have a mix of cards and some USD cash for tips, smaller vendors, and emergencies.

Health & Safety

  • Pack basic medications (pain relief, stomach aids, any prescriptions) in your carry-on.
  • Use sunscreen – coastal clouds can still burn your skin, especially midday.
  • At night, stick to busier, well-lit areas and use official rideshares or taxis.

Connectivity & Navigation

  • Consider an eSIM or local data plan rather than relying on public Wi-Fi.
  • Download offline maps and save hotel, stadium, fan zones, and airport as favorites.
  • Keep screenshots of tickets, gate info, and key addresses in a dedicated “Match Day” album.

Culture & Etiquette

  • Service staff rely heavily on tips: 15–20% is standard in restaurants and bars.
  • Be respectful when filming or taking photos in residential streets near the stadium.
  • Remember that not everyone around you is on vacation – treat neighborhoods as someone’s home, not just a fan zone.

Final Whistle: Turning a Ticket into a World-Class LA Experience

The USA’s opening match at SoFi Stadium will be one of the defining moments of FIFA World Cup 26™ – a “remember where you were” night for fans across the country. With the right planning, it can also anchor an incredible trip through one of the world’s most diverse cities.

Choose your base with intention, give yourself generous buffers on match day, respect the communities that host the tournament, and let LA’s beaches, food, and neighborhoods fill the spaces between kick-offs. Do that, and your memories of June 12, 2026 won’t just be about a final score – they’ll be about a week that felt smooth, exciting, and unforgettable from wheels down to wheels up.
