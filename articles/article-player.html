Player Watch: The Next Generation #10s

Category: Scouting Date: Oct 24, 2025 By: The Scouting Desk

The classic playmaker is evolving. With the 2026 World Cup on the horizon, we analyze three young stars redefining the iconic role for the modern era.

For a fleeting moment in the late 2010s, it felt like the "Number 10" was dying. The era of high-pressing engines and rigid tactical systems seemed to have squeezed out the luxury player—the *trequartista* who existed solely in the hole behind the striker, allergic to defensive work, waiting for the ball to arrive at their feet.

But football is cyclical. The role didn't die; it mutated.

The modern Number 10 is no longer a static passenger out of possession. They are hybrids. They are expected to press like an eight, drift wide like a winger, and possess the vision to unlock low blocks like the masters of old. They operate not just in the center, but in the "half-spaces," causing chaos between the opposition's lines.

As we look ahead to the expanded 2026 World Cup less than a year away, a new crop of creative midfielders has seized the mantle. They combine elite technical ability with the physical robustness required by modern football.

Here are three players under 23 who are defining what it means to be a playmaker in 2025.

1. The Spatial Architect: Florian Wirtz (Germany)

Age: 22 | Club: Bayer Leverkusen

If you want the blueprint for the modern attacking midfielder, look no further than Florian Wirtz. Having fully recovered from the ACL injury that threatened his early career, Wirtz has matured into arguably the most intelligent playmaker in Europe.

Wirtz doesn't just occupy space; he interprets it. He is rarely found standing still in the central channel. Instead, he constantly drifts, usually favoring the inside-left channel, dragging defensive midfielders out of position before slipping a killer through-ball to a runner. His low center of gravity allows him to turn in incredibly tight areas, making him press-resistant—a crucial trait in the modern game.

The 2026 Outlook Germany has often struggled with rigid structures in recent tournaments. Wirtz offers the antidote: pure, calculated chaos. Alongside Jamal Musiala, he forms a dual-10 partnership that is terrifying defenses globally. Wirtz will go into 2026 not as a prospect, but as the creative heartbeat of a German side looking to reclaim its throne.

2. The Pressing Playmaker: Xavi Simons (Netherlands)

Age: 22 | Club: RB Leipzig

Xavi Simons represents the most significant shift away from the classic #10 archetype. He is what happens when you put a playmaker’s brain inside a pitbull’s body.

Simons is explosive. His game is defined by high-intensity sprinting, both with and without the ball. Out of possession, he leads the press ferociously, often winning the ball back high up the pitch in dangerous areas. In possession, he is direct. He doesn't dwell on the ball; he drives at defenders, committing them before releasing a pass or taking a shot. He is comfortable playing centrally, out wide, or even as a false nine, making him a manager's dream.

The 2026 Outlook The Dutch national team has historically loved technical elegance, but modern international football requires grit. Simons brings both. He is the spark plug for the *Oranje*. In the North American heat in 2026, his engine and ability to transition from defense to attack in seconds will be vital for the Netherlands' hopes of a deep run.

3. The Silk and Steel: Arda Güler (Turkey)

Age: 20 | Club: Real Madrid

Of the three listed here, Arda Güler bears the closest resemblance to the classic #10s of the past. He is a lefty with a velvet touch, capable of moments of breathtaking aesthetic beauty—the curled shot from outside the box, the disguised reverse pass that splits a defense wide open.

However, his development at Real Madrid over the last two seasons has added necessary steel to his silk. He has learned that talent isn't enough at the elite level; you have to track back, you have to win duels, and you have to handle physical intimidation. Güler has adapted remarkably well, evolving from a luxury substitute into a player capable of dictating the tempo of high-stakes matches from the right half-space, cutting inside onto his lethal left foot.

The 2026 Outlook Turkey possesses a "golden generation" of talent, and Güler is the undisputed jewel in the crown. He is the player they look to when the system breaks down and magic is required. If Turkey is to be the dark horse of the 2026 World Cup, it will be because Arda Güler has used the tournament as his global coronation.
