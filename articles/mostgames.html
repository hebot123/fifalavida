The One-Week World Cup Plan:
How to See the Most Games in 7 Days

By FIFA La Vida Travel Advisor

If you only have one week for FIFA World Cup 26™ but want to see as many matches as possible, you don’t have to chase games all over the continent. With smart planning, you can base yourself in a single city (or region), catch 3–4 games in 7 days, and still have time to actually enjoy where you are.

Below are three high-impact strategies built around real match clusters: the “Soccer & Convenience” week in Atlanta, the “Ultimate Vacation” week in Los Angeles, and the “Maximum Games” Northeast Corridor based in Philadelphia.

One Week, Three Strategies

Option 1

Soccer & Convenience – Atlanta

3 games in 7 days, one hotel, stadium in the city center, easy indoor attractions.

Option 2

Ultimate Vacation – Los Angeles

3 games in 7 days (or 4 in 10), beaches, theme parks, and classic California summer vibes.

Option 3

Maximum Games – Northeast Corridor

4 games in 7 days across Boston, Philadelphia, and New York/New Jersey using fast trains.

The key is to think in “match clusters” instead of individual games. You’re not just picking a stadium; you’re choosing how you’ll spend the days between kickoffs.

Option 1 – “Soccer & Convenience” Week in Atlanta, USA

Why Atlanta Works So Well

If you want to maximize games with minimum hassle, Atlanta is one of the strongest one-week bases in the entire tournament.

  • 3 games in exactly 7 days (with a 4th if you extend).
  • One hotel the entire time – no repacking, no airport transfers between cities.
  • Stadium in the middle of downtown, tied directly into public transit.
  • Most major attractions are walkable or one short ride away.

The Sample Schedule (June 15–21)

Day 1 – June 15 Game 1 – Group H
Day 4 – June 18 Game 2 – Group A
Day 7 – June 21 Game 3 – Group H

Bonus: There’s an additional game on June 24 if you can stretch your trip by three days.

Where to Stay: Neighborhoods That Make the Week Easy

Downtown / Centennial Park

  • Walking distance to the stadium, aquarium, World of Coca-Cola, and civil rights museum.
  • Perfect if you want to step out of your hotel and be in the middle of everything.
  • Look for major chains around Centennial Olympic Park and Peachtree Center.

Midtown

  • A bit more local and residential, but still a quick MARTA ride or short rideshare to the stadium.
  • Great restaurants, bars, and access to the BeltLine for walks between matches.
  • Ideal if you want nightlife and a neighborhood feel, not just a conference-hotel vibe.

What to Do Between Matches

  • World of Coca-Cola: Surprisingly fun, highly branded, and fully air-conditioned – a perfect “rest your legs” attraction.
  • Georgia Aquarium: One of the largest in the world, and an easy half-day that won’t exhaust you between games.
  • National Center for Civil and Human Rights: Powerful, thoughtful, and walkable from downtown hotels and the stadium.
  • Food & markets: Explore Ponce City Market and nearby neighborhoods for everything from high-end dining to casual Southern comfort food.

Pro tip: Plan at least one “almost nothing” afternoon – just a slow lunch, a nap, and a short walk. Most fans underestimate how physically tiring a three-match week actually feels.

Option 2 – “Ultimate Vacation” Week in Los Angeles, USA

Why LA Is the Summer Holiday Version of a Match Week

If you want your World Cup trip to double as a classic summer vacation, Los Angeles is the obvious choice. In one week you can see 3 games (or 4 in 10 days), go to the beach, visit theme parks, and still have time for rooftop sunsets.

  • 3 games in 7 days (with a 4th if you extend).
  • Endless non-soccer activities within an hour of your base.
  • Great June weather – warm, sunny, and made for beaches and terraces.

The Sample Schedule (June 15–21)

Day 1 – June 15 Game 1 – Group G
Day 4 – June 18 Game 2 – Group B
Day 7 – June 21 Game 3 – Group G

Alternate plan: arrive June 12 to catch the U.S. Men’s National Team opener, then stay through June 21.

Where to Stay: Picking the Right LA Base

Beach Cities (Manhattan / Hermosa / El Segundo)

  • 25–40 minutes to the stadium outside of peak traffic (more at rush hour).
  • Perfect for a “World Cup at the beach” vibe: morning swims, evening matches.
  • Look near the piers and main streets for walkable food & nightlife.

Culver City / Westside

  • Balanced access: not far from stadium, not far from Santa Monica and Hollywood.
  • Great restaurants and bars without feeling overly touristy.
  • Strong choice if you want to explore multiple parts of LA during your week.

LAX / Stadium District

  • Airport hotels and stadium-area hotels keep logistics simple.
  • Best for ultra-practical fans: land, sleep, see games, fly out.
  • Pair with day trips to beaches or attractions when you have more time.

Santa Monica / Hollywood

  • “Postcard LA” – pier, promenade, rooftop bars, or Hollywood nightlife.
  • Longer drive to the stadium, but worth it if vacation feel is top priority.

What to Do Between Games

  • Entertainment: Universal Studios Hollywood, Warner Bros. Studio Tour, or a live TV taping.
  • Outdoors: Santa Monica Pier, Venice Beach, bike rides along the coast, or hiking to the Hollywood Sign.
  • Culture & art: The Getty Center, Getty Villa, and LACMA offer world-class collections and views.

Insider tip: Schedule the “heavy walking” days (theme parks, long hikes) for your longer gap between games, not the day before a late kickoff.

Option 3 – “Maximum Games” Week in the Northeast Corridor (Base in Philadelphia)

Why Philly + Trains Unlock a 4-Match Week

Boston, Philadelphia, and New York/New Jersey are linked by fast, frequent trains (Amtrak and regional services). If you’re willing to sit on a train for 1–3 hours, you can treat the entire Northeast Corridor as a single “super city” and chase games while sleeping in one or two main hotels.

  • Up to 4 games in 7 days across three legendary cities.
  • No domestic flights needed: trains take you city-center to city-center.
  • Flexible base: stay in Philadelphia the whole time, or split the week between two cities.

Sample Itinerary (June 13–19)

June 13 Game 1 – Boston or New York/New Jersey
June 14 Game 2 – Philadelphia
June 16 Game 3 – Boston or New York/New Jersey
June 19 Game 4 – Philadelphia or Boston

Variation: lock in two host cities (e.g., Philly + NYC) and only day-trip once to Boston to reduce hotel changes.

Why Base in Philadelphia?

  • Generally more affordable than Boston or Manhattan.
  • Easy rail hub: trains north to New York/Boston and south to other East Coast cities.
  • Compact, walkable center with historic sights and great food.

Where to Stay in Philly

  • Center City / Rittenhouse: walkable, lots of restaurants, easy transit connections.
  • Near 30th Street Station: ultra-convenient for Amtrak, still close to main sights.
  • Old City: historic feel, near Independence Hall and the Liberty Bell.

Philly Between Matches

  • Run up the “Rocky Steps” at the Art Museum (or just take the photo).
  • Visit the Liberty Bell and Independence Hall.
  • Eat your way through Reading Terminal Market and try multiple cheesesteaks.

Day-Trips by Train: New York & Boston

New York / New Jersey Games

  • Base in Philly, take an early train to NYC, then local transit to the match.
  • After the game, enjoy a late-night meal in Manhattan before heading back or stay over one night.
  • Sightseeing ideas: Central Park, Times Square, a Broadway show, or a quick museum visit.

Boston Games

  • Use one day solely for Boston: train in, game, then explore Freedom Trail or Faneuil Hall.
  • Consider a one-night stay to avoid a very late return after an evening kickoff.

Advanced move: Book train seats for non-match times (mid-morning or mid-afternoon) and plan one “no-travel” rest day in your 7-day window.

Planning Like a Pro: Tips Most Guides Don’t Mention

1. Pick Your “Base Logic” First

  • Decide which matters most: max games, minimal transfers, or vacation feel.
  • Once you pick Atlanta / LA / Philly, let that choice drive all other decisions.
  • Resist the urge to add “just one more city” if it forces an extra flight or 4 a.m. wake-ups.

2. Build in a True Rest Day

  • Three or four games in a week is physically tiring – even if you’re “just watching.”
  • Plan one day with no long transport, no big attractions, and no late-night commitments.
  • Your match-day energy (and mood) will be noticeably better by the third game.

3. Ticket & Seat Strategy

  • Think about where you sit based on your week: after long days in the sun, shade and easier exits matter more than being a few rows closer.
  • For a 4-match week, consider at least one “premium comfort” seat (club or lounge) where you can sit, eat, and recharge properly.
  • Keep all tickets stored offline and on at least two devices (e.g., your phone and a companion’s).

4. Transport Buffer Discipline

  • For every match, work backwards: “I want to be at my seat 45–60 minutes before kickoff.”
  • Add realistic time for security, walking, and unexpected delays (public transit hiccups, rideshare surges).
  • If you’re traveling by train between cities, use morning or midday slots, not last departures.

5. Pack for a One-Week “Match Capsule”

  • Bring 2–3 jerseys max, and rotate them with neutral shirts – you don’t need one for every game.
  • Prioritize footwear: one pair of broken-in sneakers or trainers that can handle 15–20k steps per day.
  • Pack a small stadium kit: clear bag (if required), power bank, light jacket, sunscreen, earplugs, and a collapsible water bottle where allowed.

One-Week World Cup Checklist

Before You Book

  • Confirm your passport validity and any visas/eTAs you need.
  • Choose your base option: Atlanta, Los Angeles, or Northeast Corridor.
  • Sketch your ideal 7-day calendar on paper: travel days, match days, and one rest day clearly marked.

After You Book

  • Save all reservations (flights, hotels, trains, tickets) in one folder or trip app.
  • Pin your hotel, stadium, and key transit hubs in your maps app for offline use.
  • Share your itinerary with a friend or family member in case you lose your phone.

Final Whistle: Choose Your Week, Then Fully Commit

Whether you choose the convenience of Atlanta, the vacation energy of Los Angeles, or the high-intensity train-hopping of the Northeast Corridor, the secret to a great one-week World Cup trip is focus. Pick your cluster, build your calendar, and resist the urge to overstuff it.

Three or four games in 7 days is plenty. Give yourself space to breathe, to explore, and to actually remember the cities you pass through – not just the scorelines. Do that, and your “one-week World Cup” will feel less like a blur and more like a perfectly edited highlight reel you’ll replay for years.
