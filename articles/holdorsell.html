If you are trying to make sense of the current ticketing chaos, I’ve compiled a timeline of what has actually happened so far and some strategy on how to handle "Right to Ticket" (RTT) tokens or lottery wins moving forward.

Here is the breakdown of ticket releases that have already occurred. If you feel like you missed the boat, you aren't alone—a significant chunk of inventory was sold "blind" before we even knew the matchups.

September 10 – September 19, 2025

Visa Presale Draw (Phase 1)

What happened: The first opportunity to apply. This was purely a "blind" draw (location/date only, no teams known).
Inventory Released: ~1 Million tickets sold.

October 27 – October 31, 2025

Early Ticket Draw (Phase 2)

What happened: A second "blind" draw for early birds.
Inventory Released: Another ~1 Million tickets sold.

Current Status: By the time the Match Schedule was revealed in December, roughly 25-30% of the total tournament inventory had already been sold.

December 5, 2025

The Final Draw

What happened: The official matchups were revealed. This changed the market instantly. "Blind" tickets suddenly had real value (or lost it) based on which teams landed in which city.

Current Phase
Dec 11, 2025 – Jan 13, 2026

What happened: This is the phase we are in now. It is the first time fans can apply for specific matchups (e.g., "I want USA vs. Italy").

Important: This window closes January 13, 2026. It is a lottery, not a race.

Part 2: Strategy – What to Hold vs. Sell

If you are lucky enough to hold "Right to Ticket" tokens (from FIFA Collect) or if you win in the upcoming lottery, here is a general rule of thumb for managing your portfolio.

A. Group Stage Matches

The "Host/Blue Chip" Strategy (HOLD)

If you hold tickets for USA, Mexico, Canada, Brazil, or Argentina, do not sell yet.

Why: Demand will skyrocket in February when the "Unsuccessful" lottery emails go out. Panic buying hasn't started yet.

The "Niche/Low Demand" Strategy (SELL NOW)

If you hold a token for a match like Curacao vs. Ivory Coast or Panama vs. Gabon in a massive stadium, sell immediately.

Why: There is a high risk these matches will NOT sell out. If tickets are available for Face Value in the Spring, your "Right to Ticket" becomes worthless.

B. Knockout Round Matches

Round of 32 / Round of 16 (HOLD): Hold these until the group stage plays out (June 2026). If a major team looks like they are heading toward your specific bracket slot, the hype will drive the price up 5x–10x before the opponent is even confirmed.

Warning: The "Category 1" Trap Be careful holding Category 1 tokens for low-demand games. The Face Value is high ($350+). Casual fans won't pay a premium on top of that for teams they don't know. Cat 1 is arguably the riskiest asset class for small games.

Part 3: Critical Upcoming Dates

January 13, 2026
Lottery Application Window Closes Last chance to apply for tickets at face value via the random draw.
February 2026
Lottery Results Announced Winners charged; losers notified. Expect resale prices to spike immediately due to FOMO.
Spring 2026
First-Come, First-Served (FCFS) Phase The "General Sale." Any unsold tickets are released. The Official FIFA Resale Platform typically launches around this time.
May/June 2026
RTT Conversion Window Crucial for Token Holders: You must manually log in and "burn" (convert) tokens into real mobile tickets or they expire.

TL;DR Summary

The easy money was made in the "Blind" phases. Now we are in the "Specific" phase. Sell the low-demand games before the general public realizes they can buy them for retail price in the Spring. Hold the big teams until the February panic sets in.
