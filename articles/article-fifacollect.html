The "Right to Buy" (RTB) System

For many users, the primary draw of FIFA Collect is the Right to Buy token. In a tournament where ticket demand outstrips supply by millions, RTBs offer a guaranteed pathway.

  • How to get them: RTBs are found in "Glory Drops" (rare, expensive packs), won through completing difficult collection "Challenges," or purchased from other users on the secondary marketplace.
  • The Conversion Process: Holding the token is not enough. You must "burn" or convert the token during specific windows (like the Nov-Dec 2025 window) to secure your seat allocation in the ticketing portal.
  • Market Value: RTBs for high-profile matches (e.g., the Final in New Jersey or the Opener in Mexico City) can trade for thousands of dollars on the marketplace.

Major 2025 Updates

1. The Blockchain Migration

Tech Update: As of mid-2025, FIFA announced a migration from the Algorand blockchain to their own FIFA Blockchain, which is EVM (Ethereum Virtual Machine) compatible.

This move is designed to make the platform compatible with popular wallets like MetaMask and improve scalability. Users who held assets on external Algorand wallets (like Pera) were required to bridge their assets back to the platform to ensure a safe transfer to the new chain.

2. FIFA Rewards (Beta)

Timed with the Club World Cup 2025, FIFA launched a "FIFA Rewards" program. This gamified loyalty layer allows fans to earn XP for interactions across the FIFA ecosystem (playing games, buying merch, collecting NFTs) to unlock real-world prizes and virtual events with football legends.

How to Get Started

  1. Create an Account: Sign up at collect.fifa.com.
  2. Browse the Marketplace: You don't always need to buy packs. The secondary marketplace allows you to snipe specific cards or RTB tokens users are selling.
  3. Complete Challenges: Check the "Challenges" tab. locking a specific set of cards (e.g., "All French Defenders from 2018") often yields rewards far more valuable than the individual cards themselves.
